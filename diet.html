Hygiene & Safety Checklist (if vet approves raw)
  • Source ingredients from reputable suppliers; keep frozen/chilled as directed.
  • Prevent cross-contamination: separate cutting boards, disinfect surfaces, wash hands/tools thoroughly.
  • Store at safe temperatures; thaw in the fridge; never leave raw meat at room temperature.
  • Keep raw foods away from children, elderly, immunocompromised individuals, and other pets.
  • Monitor stool quality, weight, coat, and energy; report concerns to your vet.
Balanced Alternatives

Consider complete & balanced cooked/commercial diets, or home-cooked plans formulated by a veterinary nutritionist. Always transition slowly between diets to avoid GI upset.

Sample Plan Concepts (Discuss with Your Vet)

  • Puppy Starter: High-quality protein, appropriate calcium/phosphorus, frequent small meals.
  • Adult Maintenance: Balanced macro- and micronutrients; adjust portions to maintain ideal body condition.
  • Active Dog: Higher calories and protein; ensure adequate omega-3s and hydration.

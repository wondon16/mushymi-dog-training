Core Cues to Start

1) Sit

  1. Hold a treat at your dog’s nose, lift it slightly over the head; as the head goes up, the bum goes down.
  2. As soon as the bum touches the ground, say “Yes!” (or click) and give the treat.
  3. Add the word “Sit” once the motion is predictable. Practice 3–5 reps, 2–3 short sessions daily.

2) Down

  1. From Sit, bring a treat from the nose straight down to the paws, then out along the floor.
  2. Mark and reward the moment elbows hit the floor. Add the cue “Down” once fluent.
  3. Reward calm duration in Down with scattered treats between paws.

3) Stay

  1. Ask for Sit. Show a flat hand “stay” signal. Count “one” silently, then mark + treat.
  2. Gradually increase duration, distance, and distractions—one at a time.
  3. Always return to the dog to reward; release with a word (“Free!”) so they learn when the stay ends.

4) Come (Recall)

  1. Start indoors: say your dog’s name happily + “Come!”, then back up a step; reward when they reach you.
  2. Play “ping-pong” recalls with two people. Keep it fun: jackpots, toys, praise.
  3. Move outdoors with a long line; gradually add distractions after success at easier levels.

5) Loose-Lead Walking

  1. Reward a “J-shape” leash—treat near your knee when the leash is slack.
  2. If the leash tightens, stop. Wait for slack to return, then move forward and reward.
  3. Practice short sessions; let sniffing be part of the reward on cue (“Go sniff”).

6) Leave It

  1. Place a low-value treat in your closed fist; when the dog disengages, mark + pay a better treat from the other hand.
  2. Put the treat on the floor covered by your hand; repeat the disengage-then-reward pattern.
  3. Uncover briefly, reward for choosing to leave it; build duration and difficulty slowly.

7) Crate & Settle

  1. Toss treats into the crate; reward any approach. Feed meals in the crate with the door open.
  2. Close door for a second, reward, open; gradually increase time, add a chew for relaxation.
  3. Use calm cues (“Settle”) and reward relaxed body language.

8) House Training (Puppies)

  1. Frequent potty breaks: after waking, eating, play, and every 60–90 minutes.
  2. Go to the same spot, quietly wait, reward big within 2 seconds of finishing.
  3. Supervise or confine between breaks; clean accidents with enzymatic cleaner.
Training Tips
  • Short, frequent sessions beat long marathons.
  • Reward generously at first; fade gradually once reliable.
  • Increase difficulty slowly; change one thing at a time (duration, distance, distraction).
  • End on a win—keep your dog eager for the next session!

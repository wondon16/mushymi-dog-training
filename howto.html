Core Cues to Start

1) Sit

  1. Lure up and back; mark “Yes!” when the bum touches ground.
  2. Add “Sit” once predictable; 3–5 reps, 2–3 short sessions daily.
  3. Reinforce calm holds of Sit to build duration.

2) Down

  1. From Sit, lure nose to floor then forward; mark when elbows down.
  2. Add cue “Down” once fluent; reward calm duration.
  3. Practice on comfy surfaces to encourage relaxation.

3) Stay

  1. Start with 1-second stays; return to dog to reward.
  2. Increase single criteria at a time: duration → distance → distractions.
  3. Use a release word (“Free!”) so your dog knows when it’s over.

4) Come (Recall)

  1. Indoors first; say name + “Come!” and back up; massive rewards at you.
  2. Two-person ping-pong recalls; then add a long line outside.
  3. Keep it fun: praise, toys, jackpots.

5) Loose-Lead Walking

  1. Pay for a slack “J” leash beside your knee.
  2. Stop when tight; move forward again when slack returns.
  3. Let sniffing be a reward on cue (“Go sniff”).

6) Leave It

  1. Reward disengagement from a covered low-value treat.
  2. Switch to better reward from the other hand.
  3. Uncover briefly; reward choosing to “leave.” Gradually increase difficulty.

7) Crate & Settle

  1. Feed near/in crate with door open; reward approaches.
  2. Close briefly, reward, open; add a chew for relaxation.
  3. Mark relaxed body language; pair with “Settle.”

8) House Training (Puppies)

  1. Breaks after waking, eating, playing; every 60–90 minutes.
  2. Reward within 2 seconds after potty outdoors.
  3. Supervise or confine; clean accidents with enzymatic cleaner.
Training Tips
  • Short, frequent sessions beat long marathons.
  • Reward generously early; fade gradually once reliable.
  • Change one factor at a time: duration, distance, or distraction.
  • End on a win to keep motivation high.
